The Last Broadcast

The Route 9 Chronicle

Alleged image of the “Route 9 Entity,” recovered from a damaged VHS recording.

October 31, 1999 — In a quiet Midwestern town, residents tuning in to KRVN 103.6 FM expected to hear the usual late-night jazz broadcast. Instead, they were met with a trembling voice reading local obituaries, each name eerily familiar to listeners still very much alive.

Police reports indicate that within minutes of the broadcast, phone lines jammed with callers insisting the voices were “layered”—multiple tones overlapping, like whispers played backward. The transmission lasted exactly 7 minutes and 13 seconds before cutting to static.

By dawn, the radio tower had gone dark. No staff were found inside the station, and the logbooks ended mid-entry. Investigators only recovered a melted reel labeled “Do Not Air – Route 9.” Witnesses claim to see the creature in this appearance right before they ate someone.

Locals refer to the event as The Last Broadcast. Every Halloween since, car radios near Route 9 flicker alive for thirteen seconds, replaying the faint hum of that night’s transmission—followed by distant breathing.

“We thought it was a prank,” one former deputy said. “But that sound... it wasn’t from this world.”
